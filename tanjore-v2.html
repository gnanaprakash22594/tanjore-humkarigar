The Art Form

Craft Born in
Royal Courts

Tanjore painting is one of India's most distinct classical art traditions, originating in the royal courts of Thanjavur during the 9th century AD. Characterised by rich, flat compositions, bold surface textures, and the lavish application of 22-karat gold foil and semi-precious stones, these works were originally created as devotional objects for temples and palaces.

The Craft

How a Tanjore Painting is Born

01
📐

Base & Sketch

Hardboard prepared with chalk powder and glue mixture. Deity sketched by hand with absolute precision — no stencils.

02
✒️

Embossing

Bas-relief carving creates the iconic heavy embossed texture — jewellery, crowns, and ornamental detail raised from the surface.

03
🥇

Gold Foil

22-karat gold leaf carefully applied to all embossed areas — the signature of authentic Tanjore work and its eternal lustre.

04
💎

Stone Setting

Semi-precious stones, glass pieces, and coloured gems hand-set to bring divine splendour to the deity's ornamentation.

05
🖼️

Framing & Delivery

Framed in premium moulding, packed with archival care and shipped to your doorstep anywhere in the world.
